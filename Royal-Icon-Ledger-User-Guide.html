The philosophy first.

Before you enter a single number

Royal-Icon Ledger is not a budgeting app. It is not a tracker. It is a financial operating system — a set of rules, thresholds, and automated decisions that govern how every unit of income you receive gets allocated before you spend it.

The system works on one core idea: give every unit a role before you spend it. Not after. Before. Income arrives → system decides where it goes → you execute. Emotion, impulse, and guesswork are removed from the equation by design.

The three pillars

Buffer first. Your family buffer is the foundation of the entire system. Nothing else grows until the floor is built. Stage 1 exists entirely to build this.
System before self. The rules you set in advance are smarter than the decisions you make under pressure. The system enforces your best intentions.
Volatility stays in the business. Trading P&L, side income, and irregular earnings never touch family life directly. The waterfall controls what flows where.

01

Onboarding.

The onboarding wizard runs automatically on first login. It takes 5–10 minutes and sets up the entire system from scratch. Do not skip it — every field matters.

Step 1 — Choose your currency

Select the currency you earn and spend in. This affects every number displayed in the app. You can change it later in Settings, but all existing numbers stay as-is — so get it right the first time.

⚠ If you earn in multiple currencies, pick your primary living currency — the one your family expenses are paid in. You can record other income in the Profit Allocator by converting it manually.

Step 2 — Income type

Variable income — income changes each month. You never know exactly what's coming. This is the primary use case the system was designed for.
Trader / Investor — income primarily from trading or investment returns. Highly irregular. The Trading P&L module is central to your workflow.
Freelancer / Contractor — project or retainer-based income. May arrive in lumps. Variable by nature.
Fixed salary — stable monthly income. Still benefits from the system — surplus allocation, envelope budgeting, and buffer building all apply.
Family / Household — managing shared or household finances as the primary controller.

Step 3 — Monthly expenses

Enter every fixed monthly cost you have. These are expenses that exist regardless of whether you earn money this month. The system pre-fills common categories — fill in the actual amounts.

✓ Be honest. If rent is R 13,000 enter R 13,000. If you have a car payment, add it. The system's accuracy depends entirely on real numbers. Underestimating your expenses leads to an unrealistically low salary figure and a buffer target that won't actually cover you.

Step 4 — Spending budget

This is the monthly amount allocated for discretionary spending — groceries, fuel, dining, personal purchases. It sits on top of your fixed expenses and feeds into your Budget envelopes.

Start with what you actually spend, not what you wish you spent. You can tighten it later once you see real data.

Step 5 — Buffer reserve

An additional monthly buffer amount set aside as part of your salary structure. Think of it as the margin between your expenses and your income floor. Set this to at least 10–15% of your total expenses.

Step 6 — Buffer target (months)

How many months of full salary should your family buffer hold? The default is 18 months. This is not conservative — it's structural. Here's the logic:

6 months — Stage 1 end (crisis floor). Minimum protection against total income loss.
12 months — Stage 1.5 end (comfort zone). A year's runway. Lifestyle begins to expand.
18 months — Stage 2 end (fortified). Full protection. Drawdown risk from business separated from family entirely.

18 months is the recommended target. You can reduce it if your income is truly stable, but most variable-income earners need every month of it.

Step 7 — Starting balances

Enter what you actually have right now across your accounts:

Family Buffer
Your emergency / family savings account. This is the money sitting in a savings account that is not touched for trading or investment. If you have R 50,000 in a savings account dedicated to family security, enter that.
Trading Capital
Capital deployed in trading, not your family savings. If you have R 20,000 in your trading account, enter that here. Do not mix this with family savings.
Long-term
Money in index funds, retirement accounts, or long-term investments. Separate from both buffer and trading. If zero, enter zero — it will grow through the waterfall.

Step 8 — Override PIN (optional)

A 4-digit PIN that locks your system configuration. When set, editing expenses, balances, and rules requires entering the PIN. Protects against impulsive changes to your own system during high-emotion moments. Highly recommended for traders.

Step 9 — Notifications

Enable push notifications to receive daily financial check-in reminders, weekly pulse reviews, and monthly review prompts. Set your preferred morning and evening notification times.

02

Setup & Salary.

This is the foundation of the entire system. Everything else — your salary, buffer target, stage thresholds, and allocation amounts — is computed from what you enter here.

How salary is computed

Monthly Salary = Total Expenses + Spending Budget + Buffer Reserve

You do not enter your salary directly. The system computes it from your real costs. This means your "salary" is the minimum you need to earn each month to cover everything and build the buffer.

Monthly expenses

Add every recurring fixed cost. Click any amount to edit it. Use the category dropdown so the system can group and analyse your cost structure.

What counts as a monthly expense

Rent or bond repayment
Utilities (electricity, water, internet)
Insurance premiums (car, life, health)
School fees or childcare
Family support obligations
Subscriptions (Netflix, software, gym)
Car payments or transport costs
⚠ Do not add groceries or dining here — those go into your spending budget and then into Budget envelopes. Only add costs that are the same every month regardless of what you do.

The three computed numbers

Total Expenses
Sum of all your fixed monthly costs. This is what leaves your account automatically every month no matter what.
Monthly Salary
The minimum you need to earn. This is your system's definition of income security. When the Profit Allocator says your buffer covers 5.6 months, it means 5.6 × this number.
Buffer Target
Monthly Salary × your target months (default 18). This is the total amount your Family Buffer needs to reach before Stage 2 is complete. It is your finish line.
ℹ Update your expenses whenever your life changes — new rent, new insurance, new subscription. The system recalculates everything instantly. The numbers should always reflect reality, not aspirations.
03

Command Dashboard.

This is your daily home screen. It shows your current stage, balance positions, progression toward the buffer target, and your action queue.

The Stage Card

The large card at the top tells you exactly where you are in the system. Understanding the stages is critical:

Stage 1
Build the Floor
Buffer: R 0 → 6 months salary. 100% of profit goes to buffer. No lifestyle. No trading expansion. Just the floor.
Stage 1.5
Growing Cushion
Buffer: 6 → 12 months. 55% buffer / 30% long-term / 15% goals. Long-term investing begins. Light lifestyle unlocked.
Stage 2
Final Push
Buffer: 12 → 18 months. 65% buffer / 20% long-term / 15% goals. Heavy push to completion.
Stage 3
Full Waterfall
Buffer complete. 30% long-term / 30% trading / 20% goals / 20% lifestyle. Full wealth-building mode.

Current Balances

This section shows your five financial positions. Click Unlock to edit to update them. Do this whenever a balance changes — after a profit allocation, after a transfer, after a withdrawal.

Trading Capital
Your active trading/investment account balance. This is capital at risk. Update it after every trading period. The Trading P&L module reads from this to compute drawdown.
Family Buffer
Your protected family savings account. This is the most important number in the system. Update it after every profit allocation that routes money here. Never withdraw from this except for a genuine emergency.
Monthly Salary
Auto-computed from your Setup & Salary expenses. Read-only. This is what the system considers your monthly income requirement.
Long-term
Your index funds, retirement account, or passive investment balance. Update after each contribution. This grows through waterfall allocations in Stage 1.5 and beyond.
Future Goals
Total saved toward named goals (car, deposit, business). The individual goals are defined in Setup. This balance tracks the total pool. Update when you move money toward a goal.

Snapshots — your financial history

Hit "Save snapshot" after updating your balances. A snapshot locks in today's position: buffer amount, trading capital, long-term balance, net worth, and salary at that point in time.

Take a snapshot at minimum once a month — ideally after every significant income event. Over time, your History tab will show your net worth growth and the rate at which you're building the buffer.

✓ Snapshots are the memory of your financial life. They cannot be edited after saving. Take them regularly — you will want to look back at how far you've come.
04

Budget.

The Budget tab uses envelope budgeting — your spending money is divided into named buckets, each with its own cap and enforcement rules. When you log a purchase through the Spending Gate, it gets assigned to an envelope and reduces its balance.

Setting up envelopes

On first use, the system suggests envelopes based on your expenses and spending budget. Accept, adjust, or rebuild from scratch. Your total envelope caps should roughly match your spending budget.

Suggested envelope structure

Survival — groceries, household basics, fuel. Largest envelope. Soft warning mode (can't block essentials).
Personal — clothing, dining out, personal care. Hard block or PIN override — you want friction here.
Emergency — unexpected expenses within the month. Roll over mode — unspent accumulates for when you need it.
Family — kids, activities, family outings. Soft warning — family needs flexibility.

Block modes — enforcement rules

Soft warning — shows a warning when over budget but allows the purchase. Use for essential envelopes (groceries) where you cannot practically refuse to spend.
Hard block — refuses purchases that would exceed the cap. The gate literally says no. Use for discretionary envelopes where you want a firm ceiling.
PIN override — hard block that can be bypassed with your 4-digit PIN. The highest level of friction. Use for your most dangerous spending categories. The PIN forces a conscious decision.

Rollover modes — what happens to unspent money

Reset — use it or lose it. Cap resets to full at month start. Good for dining, entertainment — prevents money accumulating in places you want to control.
Roll over — unspent carries to next month. Good for emergency or irregular expense envelopes. If you spend less in January, February gets extra room.
Sweep to buffer — unspent goes directly into your Family Buffer at month end. The wealth-building option. Use this on your Personal envelope if you want to incentivise underspending.

Reading the velocity indicator

Each envelope shows a pace indicator based on day of month versus spend rate:

On pace — at current spending rate, you'll end the month within budget. Green.
Over pace — projected end-of-month spend exceeds the cap. Slow down now. Orange.
Over budget — cap already exceeded this month. No further spending in this envelope.
05

Profit Allocator.

This is the core wealth-building engine. Every time you receive income — a trading profit, a client payment, a salary deposit — you run it through the Profit Allocator. The system reserves taxes first, then routes the remainder based on your current stage rules.

⚠ Run every income event through this. Every single one. This is not optional — it is the mechanism by which your buffer grows, your long-term investments compound, and your stage progresses. Skipping it means your system is not actually operating.

How to use it — step by step

  • 1
    Navigate to Profit Allocator tab. Confirm which stage you're in (shown at the top of the page).
  • 2
    Enter your gross income for this event — the full amount before tax. If you received R 15,000, enter 15000.
  • 3
    The live preview shows exactly where every unit goes based on your current stage rules and tax reserve percentage.
  • 4
    Click Allocate → to confirm. The system records the allocation and prepares to apply the changes.
  • 5
    Click Apply to balances to add the allocated amounts to your actual balance positions.
  • 6
    Physically transfer the money. Move the buffer portion to your savings account. Move the tax reserve to a separate account. The system tells you what to move — you execute it in your actual bank.
  • 7
    Go to Command Dashboard → Save snapshot to record this moment in your history.

The allocation waterfall — what happens to your money

Stage 1 allocation (default)

Tax Reserve (25% of gross) — Set aside first, before anything else. Move this to a separate account for your tax obligation. Adjust the percentage in Rules to match your actual tax rate.
Family Buffer (100% of net) — In Stage 1, every cent of net profit goes to the buffer. No exceptions. This is how the floor gets built.

Stage 1.5 allocation (default)

Tax Reserve (25%)
Family Buffer (55%) — still the priority
Long-term (30%) — index funds / retirement investing begins here
Goals (15%) — saving toward named goals

Stage 3 allocation (default — full waterfall)

Tax Reserve (25%)
Long-term (30%)
Trading Capital (30%)
Goals (20%)
Lifestyle (20%) — this is what you spend on yourself
✓ All stage percentages are fully editable in the Rules tab. The defaults are designed for a trading/variable-income earner with family obligations. Adjust them to match your actual situation.
06

Trading P&L.

This tab tracks your trading performance and enforces risk management rules. The key principle: volatility stays in the business. What happens in your trading account should never directly affect your family's life.

Capital field

Update this to your current trading account balance whenever it changes. This is the base from which drawdown is calculated. Click the lock icon to unlock and edit it.

Logging monthly P&L

At the end of each trading month (or whenever you want to record a result), scroll down to the Monthly P&L section and log the result — positive or negative. The system tracks your win rate, year-to-date P&L, and average win/loss over time.

The Drawdown Protocol

This is your automated risk management system. It tracks how far your trading capital has dropped from its peak (high water mark) and changes your allowed behaviour based on the severity.

Normal (0–9%)
Full position sizes. Trade your plan. You are within normal variance. No changes required.
Caution (10–19%)
Reduce position sizes by 25%. Review your last 10 trades for pattern issues. Something may be off with the market or your execution.
Defensive (20–29%)
Reduce position sizes by 50%. Consider a week off. Review strategy assumptions. This is a significant drawdown — the system is protecting you from digging deeper.
Stop (30%+)
Stop trading. Full pause required. A mandatory strategy review before resuming. This is not a suggestion — it is a rule. Continuing to trade through a 30%+ drawdown is how accounts get destroyed.
⚠ The drawdown protocol only works if you update your trading capital balance regularly. If you don't keep it current, the system cannot protect you. Update it after every meaningful P&L event.

When to run income through the Profit Allocator

When you withdraw profit from your trading account to your main account — run it through the Profit Allocator. The trading capital balance in this tab should then be reduced by the amount you withdrew. The Profit Allocator then routes it to buffer, long-term, etc. based on your stage.

07

Impulse Control.

The Impulse Control module is your spending filter. Before making any discretionary purchase above your gate threshold, you run it through the gate. The system evaluates it against your rules and your current financial state, then either approves, warns, or blocks it.

Spending Gate

The primary tool. Every purchase above your threshold (default: any discretionary purchase) goes through here before you spend the money.

What is it?
Name the item you want to buy. Be specific — "New phone" not "tech thing". Specificity forces clarity of intent.
Amount
The full cost of the purchase including any fees, delivery, or extras.
Envelope
Which budget envelope does this come from? This is where the enforcement rules apply. If the envelope is hard-blocked and over budget, the gate refuses the purchase.
Category
The type of purchase — food, clothes, tech, family, etc. Used for tracking your impulse patterns over time.

After you submit, the gate checks your envelope balance, your current drawdown status, and your buffer protection status. It returns one of three outcomes: Approved, Warning (soft-blocked envelope), or Blocked (hard-blocked or PIN envelope).

Quick Log

For purchases you've already made and need to record fast. Enter the amount, category, and envelope — it logs directly to your spending history without going through the gate approval process. Use this for cash purchases, small amounts, or anything you've already committed to.

History & Triggers

Every purchase run through the gate is logged here with its outcome, category, and trigger. Over time you'll see patterns: which categories you impulse-buy most, which emotional states trigger spending, and how your gate decisions correlate with your drawdown levels.

Emotional triggers — why they matter

When you log a purchase, you can optionally select the trigger — what emotional state drove it. Options include: Bored, Stressed, Tired, Won a trade, Lost a trade, Family pressure, Scrolling, Saw an ad.

This data is for you, not the system. After 2–3 months, the pattern becomes clear: most people have 1–2 dominant triggers. Knowing yours is the first step to catching it.

History.

Your financial timeline. Every snapshot you've saved appears here as a data point on the net worth chart and as a row in the snapshot list.

Net worth over time chart

Shows the stacked growth of your three main positions: Buffer, Trading Capital, and Long-term investments. The chart is only meaningful if you take snapshots regularly — each snapshot is one data point.

All snapshots

Each snapshot shows the date, the three balance positions at that moment, and the total net worth. You can delete a snapshot if it was taken in error. Snapshots cannot be edited.

ℹ Take snapshots on a consistent schedule — the first of every month, or immediately after every income allocation. Consistency matters more than frequency. One snapshot per month for 12 months gives you a powerful picture of trajectory.

Monthly P&L chart

A bar chart of your trading P&L by month (logged in the Trading P&L tab). Shows wins, losses, and overall YTD performance. Use this to identify seasonal patterns and strategy decay over time.

Rules.

The Rules tab is where you configure the underlying mechanics of the allocation system. Most users set these once and leave them. Change them only if your situation materially changes — not because you're tempted by a better month.

Tax Reserve %

The percentage of gross income set aside for tax before any allocation. The default is 25% — a conservative rate suitable for most self-employed earners in moderate tax jurisdictions.

How to set your tax reserve correctly

Talk to a tax advisor and get your estimated effective tax rate for the year.
Add 3–5% as a buffer above that rate — tax bills are rarely lower than estimated, often higher.
Set the reserve to that combined number. Example: 22% effective rate + 4% buffer = 26% reserve.
Move every tax reserve amount to a dedicated account immediately after allocation. Never let it sit in your trading or spending account.

Profit allocation by stage

Each stage has five allocation percentages. Each row must total exactly 100%. The system does not enforce this for you — if your percentages don't add up, your allocation will be incorrect.

Buffer %
Percentage of net profit routed to your Family Buffer. Should be highest in early stages, dropping to 0% once Stage 2 is complete (buffer is full).
Long-term %
Percentage going to index funds, retirement accounts, or long-term passive investments. Increases as the buffer fills.
Trading %
Percentage reinvested into trading capital. Only meaningful in Stage 3 — before that, trading growth should come from retained profits, not new allocations.
Goals %
Percentage toward named future goals (house deposit, car, business). Active from Stage 1.5 onward.
Lifestyle %
Percentage for personal discretionary spending beyond the base budget. This is your reward for the system working. Only meaningful in Stage 3.
⚠ Do not increase Lifestyle % before Stage 3. The system allows it mechanically, but doing so before your buffer is complete means you are spending the money your family needs. The stages exist for a reason.

Stage thresholds

The buffer amounts at which stages change. By default:

Stage 1 ends at 6 months × monthly salary
Stage 1.5 ends at 12 months × monthly salary
Stage 2 ends at your buffer target (default 18 months × salary)

Buffer protect mode

When enabled, buffer protect activates if your buffer drops below the protect threshold (default: 16 months). The system temporarily reverts to Stage 1 allocation rules — 100% of profits go back to buffer — until the threshold is recovered. This protects against erosion of a hard-built buffer.

Settings.

Account tab

Display Name
The name shown in the app header. Set it to whatever you want — your name, a title, a nickname. It does not affect any system data.
Password
Update your login password here. You stay signed in on the current device after changing it.
Currency
Change your display currency. Note: this changes how numbers are displayed, not the numbers themselves. If you switch from ZAR to USD, R 120,000 becomes $ 120,000 — the amount does not convert.
Income Type
Your income profile. Affects which modules are shown (e.g., Trading P&L is hidden for fixed-salary users) and how the Profit Allocator labels things.
Override PIN
Set or change the 4-digit PIN that locks system configuration. Leave blank to disable PIN protection.

Sessions tab

View all active sessions across your devices. Sign out individual sessions remotely — useful if you've logged in on a shared device or want to revoke access from an old phone.

Access tab (admin only)

Generate and manage invite codes for new users. This panel is only visible to authenticated users (you).

Generate code
Creates a random 6-character invite code. Optionally tie it to a specific email address so only that person can use it. The code appears in the list immediately — copy it and send it to the invitee.
Active codes
All unused codes. Copy, share, or delete them from here.
Used codes
Codes that have been claimed. Shows which email used each code and when. You can reset a used code if someone had trouble signing up.
Access requests
Users who requested access from the app's login screen. Approve to auto-generate a tied code. Reject to dismiss the request.

Data & Sync tab

Cloud sync
Your data syncs to the cloud automatically every 2 seconds after any change. The sync status (synced / syncing / failed) is shown at the top of this tab and in the bottom-right corner of the app.
Export data
Downloads a full JSON export of all your financial data. Store this as a backup. Useful if you want to migrate, audit, or analyse your data outside the app.
Import data
Restore from a previous export. Use this to migrate from the local-only version to the cloud version, or to recover from a corrupted session.

Danger zone tab

Contains destructive actions — account deletion and data wipe. These cannot be undone. Export your data before using anything in this section.

Quick Reference

Monthly workflow.

This is the recommended operating rhythm. Run through it every time income arrives.

  1. 1
    Income arrives — client pays, trade profits, salary deposits.
  2. 2
    Open Profit Allocator — enter the gross amount. Review the live preview. Click Allocate → Apply.
  3. 3
    Transfer physically — move the buffer portion to savings. Move the tax reserve to its dedicated account. Move long-term allocation to your investment account.
  4. 4
    Update balances — Command → Unlock to edit → update each balance to its new actual amount.
  5. 5
    Save snapshot — lock in this moment. Your history updates.
  6. 6
    Check stage — are you closer to the next threshold? What changed?
  7. 7
    Continue operating — use the Spending Gate for discretionary purchases. Log trading results as they happen.

The single most important habit

Run every income event through the Profit Allocator and transfer the money immediately. Don't let it sit in your account without a role. Money without a role gets spent. The system only works if you execute it.
