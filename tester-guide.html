01 Welcome

Welcome & Purpose

What this beta is for

Royal Ledger is a personal finance operating system. Not a budgeting app, not a spending tracker — a structured decision-making system for people who want real control over their money. It has rules, gates, and deliberate friction built in, because that is the point. This beta is about finding out whether that philosophy works in practice, for real people, in real financial situations.

You are here because you were selected. This is a closed beta — voucher-compensated, invite-only, and deliberately small. We want depth of feedback, not breadth of users. Your experience as a tester is more valuable than a thousand installs from people who tap through an app once and forget it exists.

What we are testing
  • Whether the four profiles are clearly differentiated and correctly matched to real income situations
  • Whether the Spending Gate (Impulse Control) actually changes financial decisions, or just adds friction for friction's sake
  • Whether the Allocator logic makes sense to real users, or requires more explanation
  • Whether Envelope Budgeting, Daily Checkpoints, and Weekly Pulse are used consistently — and if not, why not
  • Whether the app's language, labels, and navigation are clear enough to use without a manual
  • Whether cloud sync, snapshots, and backup/restore work reliably across devices
  • Whether Royal Ledger feels like a system people want to return to, or a novelty they abandon after a week
What we are not testing yet

Some features are intentionally absent from this build. Do not report the following as bugs — they are planned for a later build and are noted as known limitations in Section 8:

  • In-app push notifications and reminders
  • Bank statement or transaction import
  • Shared household or multi-user mode
  • In-app subscription management or billing
  • Analytics and historical spending trend charts

03 Profile Guide

Choosing your profile

Royal Ledger has four profiles. Your profile determines which features are available to you, how the Allocator works, and what the app expects of your income situation. Pick the one that honestly reflects how your money arrives — not what you wish it looked like.

If you are genuinely unsure between two profiles, pick the simpler one. You can change your profile by contacting the owner — it is not a permanent decision, but it does reset some of your configuration.

foundation
🌱
Building from Zero
Foundation
For people starting from scratch — building the habit of saving before worrying about investment or income optimisation. Includes the Foundation Arc: a four-stage progression from Starter through to Complete. Simplified Money Allocator. No trading features.
Best for you if: You don't currently have 3 months of expenses saved, or you're rebuilding after a financial reset and want to build discipline before complexity.
fixed
💼
Salary
Salary
For people on a steady monthly paycheck. Income is predictable, so the focus is on surplus allocation — where the money left after expenses goes. Includes the Surplus Allocator (buffer → long-term savings → goals → lifestyle). No Trading P&L tab.
Best for you if: You receive a consistent salary every month and your income doesn't fluctuate significantly. You want to systematise where your surplus goes.
variable
📈
Trading / Self-Employed
Trading / Self-Employed
For people whose income changes month to month — traders, freelancers, contractors. Full feature set: Trading P&L tab, Drawdown Protocol (Normal / Caution / Defensive / Stop zones), Emotional Trading Guard (24h cool-down on loss days), and Profit Allocator with tax reserve first.
Best for you if: Your income varies significantly month to month, you trade or run a business, and you need rules around both income variability and emotional decision-making.
mixed
⚡
Hybrid
Hybrid
For people with a steady salary plus business or side income. Tracks both income streams separately. Profit Allocator with full stage rules and a capital allocation pool for business reinvestment. No Trading P&L tab — "capital" replaces "trading capital" in the waterfall.
Best for you if: You have a day job AND a side business, freelance income, or rental income that you also want to systematically allocate.
Foundation Arc note: If you choose the Foundation profile, you will progress through four stages as your savings build: Starter → Established → Stable → Complete. When you reach Complete (12+ months of expenses saved), you can graduate to any other profile. This progression is tracked automatically based on your balance inputs.
04 Getting Started

Four steps to get going

1
Register and sign in
Open the app and create your account using your email address. You'll receive a verification link — click it to confirm your account. Once verified, sign in. Your data will sync to the cloud automatically from this point forward. If you plan to test on multiple devices, sign in with the same account on each device to verify sync works correctly.
2
Complete onboarding
After sign-in, you'll be taken through onboarding. Select your profile (see Section 3), choose your currency, and enter your current financial starting position — existing savings, monthly income, and any existing long-term savings or goals. Do this honestly. The Allocator and stage progress only make sense if your starting numbers are real. Don't skip this step or enter placeholder values.
3
Set up your spending envelopes
Go to the Budget tab and add your regular monthly expenses as envelopes. Name them with categories that match your real spending: Groceries, Fuel, Eating Out, Clothing, Entertainment — whatever applies to your life. Set realistic monthly caps for each. This is what the Spending Gate and block system use to evaluate purchases. If your envelopes don't reflect reality, the blocks won't make sense. Take 10–15 minutes to do this properly before testing other features.
4
Log your first snapshot
Go to the Command tab and take your first balance snapshot. Enter your current balances across all fields shown — Buffer/Savings, Salary or Money Available, Long-term savings, and any Goals. This establishes your starting net worth. From this point on, you have a baseline to compare against. Take a snapshot at least once a week throughout the beta — this is one of the four criteria for your voucher and one of the most important habits the app is designed to build.
First use tip: After completing these four steps, open the Impulse Control tab and add one item — even a small, hypothetical purchase — just to see how the Spending Gate works before you need it for a real decision. Understanding the flow in advance makes it much more likely you'll actually use it when an impulse arrives.
05 Features

Core features to test

The following features represent the core of Royal Ledger's value proposition. For each one, we've noted what we need you to specifically look for and report on. You don't need to test everything on day one — work through features over the four-week period as they become relevant to your actual financial decisions.

Feature 01
Spending Gate
(Impulse Control)
Impulse Control tab

The Spending Gate is the core behavioural friction feature. When you feel an impulse to buy something unplanned, you log it here: name the item, enter the amount, and select your emotional trigger (Bored, Stressed, Tired, Scrolling, Saw an ad, Family pressure — plus Won a trade / Lost a trade for Variable profile). The app shows you the cost in hours of work and the 30-year investment value of that money. A 24-hour waiting period then begins. After 24 hours, you decide: approve or abandon.

What to look for
  • Does the trigger list feel accurate and complete, or are options missing?
  • Did the "hours of work" or "30-year value" numbers change how you felt about the purchase?
  • Is the 24-hour wait enforced correctly — and does it count from when you log it?
  • After you make a decision (buy / abandon), does it feel resolved?
  • Does anything in the flow feel like an annoying obstacle rather than useful friction?
Feature 02
Envelope
Budgeting
Budget tab

Envelopes are named spending categories with monthly caps. Each envelope tracks spending against its limit. When you reach the cap, the envelope can either hard-block the expense or allow a PIN override (depending on your configuration). At month end, you can roll over remaining budget, sweep it to savings, or reset to zero.

What to look for
  • Is envelope creation clear — naming, limit-setting, mode selection (hard block vs PIN override)?
  • If you hit a hard block, does it feel like it worked — or like an error message?
  • If you used the PIN override, did entering the PIN make you reconsider?
  • Are the month-end rollover and sweep options clear and easy to action?
  • Does the budget overview give you a clear picture of where you are vs. your limits?
Feature 03
Allocator
Allocator tab

The Allocator routes your surplus or profit to the right financial buckets in the right order. Foundation uses the Money Allocator (simplified). Salary uses the Surplus Allocator (buffer → long-term → goals → lifestyle). Trading/Self-employed and Hybrid use the Profit Allocator (tax reserve → buffer → long-term → capital → goals → lifestyle). The waterfall fills each bucket to its configured level before moving to the next.

What to look for
  • Does the waterfall logic match your mental model of how money should flow?
  • Is it clear what each stage is doing and why — or does the output feel like a black box?
  • When you run the allocator, does the result feel correct for your situation?
  • Can you adjust the parameters (buffer target, long-term contribution, etc.) easily?
  • Foundation only: does the stage progression feel motivating or arbitrary?
Feature 04
Daily
Checkpoints
Ritual card

Five daily financial habits presented as a ritual card. Designed to take 2–3 minutes to complete. The checkpoints build the micro-habits that support the larger system — reviewing your balances, checking your envelopes, logging any spending decisions, confirming your daily impulse state. Completing all five marks the day as done.

What to look for
  • Do the five habits feel meaningful or like arbitrary box-ticking?
  • Are any habits confusing — unclear what "completing" them actually means?
  • How long does the full ritual actually take you?
  • Do you feel any different after completing the checkpoints, or is it purely mechanical?
  • What would make you more likely to remember to do this every day?
Feature 05
Weekly Pulse
Weekly Pulse

A weekly financial check-in covering income received, expenses logged, financial mood, and a short reflection. Designed to take 5–10 minutes. The Pulse creates a structured weekly review rhythm — the equivalent of a weekly financial meeting with yourself. Includes a mood indicator and a space for written reflection.

What to look for
  • Does the Weekly Pulse feel like it belongs in a finance app, or does the mood/reflection component feel out of place?
  • Are the questions asked during the Pulse relevant to your real week?
  • How long does it actually take to complete?
  • After completing a Pulse, do you feel like you have a clearer picture of your week?
  • Is the trigger to do the Pulse obvious — do you know when and how to access it?
Feature 06
Snapshot &
Net Worth
Command tab

The snapshot system captures a point-in-time record of all your balances and calculates net worth as the sum. Snapshots build a history over time so you can see financial progress. The Command tab shows current balances, snapshot history, and stage progress for your profile.

What to look for
  • Is the net worth calculation clear — do you understand what's included and how it's summed?
  • Does entering your balances feel intuitive, or are the field labels confusing?
  • After taking multiple snapshots, does the history view show progress meaningfully?
  • Does seeing your net worth number motivate you or feel irrelevant?
  • Is the snapshot something you would naturally do weekly without being reminded?
Feature 07
Cloud Sync
(Multi-device)
All tabs

Royal Ledger syncs your data in real time via Supabase. Sign in on a second device using the same account and your data should appear immediately. This feature is critical infrastructure — if sync breaks, the whole app is fragile. We need to know if anything fails to appear on the second device, appears with a delay, or shows conflicting data.

What to look for
  • Sign in on a second device (phone + tablet, or phone + computer browser). Does your data appear?
  • Make a change on one device — add a Spending Gate item, take a snapshot — and check if it appears on the other device within 30 seconds.
  • Are there any data fields that don't sync or show old values on the second device?
  • Does signing out and back in correctly restore your data?
06 Feedback

How to submit feedback

The feedback channel

All feedback goes via WhatsApp to the owner. This is intentional — it keeps the feedback loop direct, fast, and personal. There is no ticketing system, no feedback form, no intermediary. You message, it gets read, it gets acted on.

There are two types of feedback messages:

  • Structured: Your responses to the weekly Q&A questions and pulse check-ins (see the Tester Q&A Programme document). These are sent on a schedule.
  • Ad hoc: Any bug, confusion, suggestion, or reaction you want to share in the moment. Send these as they happen — don't queue them up for later.
What to include in an ad hoc message

For any issue or observation, include the following in your message:

  • Which tab or screen you were on (e.g. "Budget tab", "Impulse Control", "Command")
  • What you were trying to do (e.g. "I was trying to add a new envelope")
  • What you expected to happen (e.g. "I expected it to save and show in the list")
  • What actually happened (e.g. "It showed a blank screen and the envelope didn't appear")
  • Your profile if relevant (Foundation, Salary, Trading/Self-employed, Hybrid)

Screenshots are always welcome and often more useful than descriptions. If something looks wrong visually, screenshot it immediately.

Please keep feedback separate from general questions. If you have a question about how a feature works, ask it — but start with "Quick question:" so it's flagged differently from bug reports. Mixing support questions and feedback reports in the same message makes it harder to triage.
07 Quality

What makes good feedback

Good feedback is specific, contextual, and honest. It tells us exactly what happened, where it happened, and what you felt about it. The table below shows examples of the difference between useful and not useful feedback across common categories.

Not useful Useful Why
"The app is confusing." "The Allocator output shows three numbers but I don't know which one is the action I need to take — the labels aren't clear enough." Specific screen, specific problem, actionable.
"The Spending Gate doesn't work." "I logged an impulse on Tuesday evening and on Wednesday morning the 24h timer had reset to zero. I have a screenshot." Reproducible, with evidence.
"I like it." "After running the allocator for the first time, I actually moved money to long-term savings the same day — which I haven't done in months. The waterfall made it feel obvious." Positive feedback with a specific behavioural result is just as useful.
"Add dark mode." "The envelope list in the Budget tab is hard to read in direct sunlight — the contrast on the balance numbers feels too low." Describes an actual problem rather than assuming a solution.
"The numbers are wrong." "My net worth shows R45,000 but when I add up the individual balances manually it comes to R48,500. I think the goals balance isn't being included." Gives us the data we need to reproduce and fix it.
"I don't use that feature." "I haven't used the Daily Checkpoints because I open the app at night before bed, and by then the habit tracking feels pointless — I want to do it in the morning but I keep forgetting." Non-use with a reason is just as valuable as use with feedback.
Rule of thumb: If your feedback could apply to any app — not just Royal Ledger specifically — it's probably not specific enough. "It's slow" could mean anything. "The snapshot save takes about 4 seconds and shows a blank screen while waiting, which made me think it had crashed" is specific enough to act on.
08 Limitations

Known limitations — not bugs

The following are intentional gaps in this version of Royal Ledger. They are on the product roadmap but not yet built. Please do not report these as bugs — if you would like to express a priority preference for any of them, you are welcome to include that in your feedback, but know that they are already known and planned.

  • NOT YET
    In-app push notifications and reminders. The app cannot currently send you a notification to complete your daily checkpoints, remind you to log a snapshot, or alert you when a 24-hour Spending Gate wait has ended. Reminders must be set manually in your phone's calendar or alarm for now.
  • NOT YET
    Bank statement or transaction import. There is no way to import transactions from your bank, upload a statement, or connect to a banking API. All entries are manual. This is intentional in the first version — automated import can create a passive relationship with money that works against the app's purpose — but it is under review for future builds.
  • NOT YET
    Shared household or multi-user mode. Royal Ledger currently supports one user per account. There is no way to link two accounts as a household, share envelopes with a partner, or view combined net worth across two users. Household mode is planned for a later version.
  • NOT YET
    Recurring expense automation. Envelopes must be manually reset and managed each month. The app does not automatically deduct recurring bills from envelopes, schedule recurring entries, or auto-reset caps at month-end. Month-end actions are currently user-initiated.
  • NOT YET
    Analytics and spending trend charts. There are no charts, graphs, or historical spending trend visualisations in this version. The snapshot history shows net worth over time, but there is no spending category breakdown, income trend line, or envelope spend history view yet.
  • NOT YET
    In-app subscription or payment management. There is no billing system inside the app. Beta access is granted manually by the owner. Post-beta pricing, subscription management, and payment processing are not yet built.
09 FAQ

Frequently asked questions

Can I change my profile after I've set up the app?
Yes, but not from inside the app in this version — contact the owner via WhatsApp. Changing profiles may affect your Allocator settings and Foundation Arc stage progress. Your snapshot history and envelope data are preserved.
What currency should I use?
Use whatever currency you actually transact in. Royal Ledger supports ZAR, USD, GBP, EUR, and others. Select your currency during onboarding. Currency is locked after onboarding — if you need to change it, contact the owner. All numbers in the app will display in your chosen currency.
What happens to my data at the end of the beta?
Your data is not deleted at the end of the beta period. The intention is that beta accounts will convert to regular accounts when the app launches publicly. You will not need to start over. If anything changes about this, you will be notified before it happens.
I found a bug. How urgently should I report it?
Report it as soon as you notice it. Don't queue bugs — fresh context makes them much easier to reproduce. If it's something that blocks you from using the app at all, start your message with "BLOCKER:" to flag it as high priority. If it's a visual glitch or minor issue, a regular message is fine.
The 24-hour Spending Gate wait seems too long. Can I skip it?
The 24-hour wait is intentional and is core to the feature's purpose. There is no skip — that would defeat the point. If you feel 24 hours is the wrong duration for a particular type of purchase, that is exactly the kind of feedback we want. Tell us what the item was, what the amount was, and why the wait felt wrong for that situation.
Do I need to log every single purchase through the Spending Gate?
No. The Spending Gate is for unplanned, impulse-driven purchases — things that weren't in your envelopes or budget. Regular, planned expenses go through your envelopes in the Budget tab. Think of the Gate as a filter for spending decisions that would normally happen on impulse without a second thought.
What if I genuinely can't complete the minimum activity criteria this month?
Life happens. If you're unable to complete the criteria due to circumstances — illness, travel, a difficult month — message the owner and let us know. We will not automatically disqualify anyone who communicates proactively. What we can't accommodate is silence — if you go quiet for the full beta period with no activity and no communication, the voucher won't be issued.
Can I invite someone else to join the beta?
Not without checking first. This is a closed beta and the number of testers is deliberately managed. If you know someone who would be an excellent tester — particularly someone with a genuinely different income profile or situation than yours — mention their name to the owner. They may be considered for a future cohort.
I'm on Foundation. When do I graduate to another profile?
The Foundation Arc reaches "Complete" status when you have 12 or more months of expenses saved and have been using the app for at least 30 days. At that point, you'll see a graduation option in the app to move to Salary, Trading/Self-employed, or Hybrid. Graduation is voluntary — you are not forced to leave Foundation.
How is my net worth calculated?
Net worth in Royal Ledger is the sum of all the balance fields you enter in a snapshot: Buffer/Savings + Monthly Salary or Money Available + Long-term savings + Future Goals (combined) + Capital (for Hybrid and Trading profiles). It is a simple sum of what you've declared — the app does not connect to your bank and does not know about liabilities or debts unless you represent them as negative values in a balance field.
