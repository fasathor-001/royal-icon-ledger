5-Minute Quick Start
Five steps to get from sign-in to your first real action in the app.
1
Open the app and sign in.

Go to royalledger.app. Use the email address your invite code was sent to — the code is tied to that email and won't work on another. If your code has expired (30-day limit), email support@royalledger.app.

2
Complete onboarding — choose your profile and currency carefully.

You'll be asked for your income profile (Foundation, Variable, Fixed, or Mixed), your currency, your salary, your fixed monthly expenses, and your current buffer balance. Both profile and currency are locked after setup — they cannot be self-changed.

3
Check your stage on the Command tab.

After onboarding the app places you in a buffer stage (1, 1.5, 2, or 3) based on your buffer vs. your target. Confirm the stage shown makes sense for your numbers.

Go to the History tab and take a monthly snapshot. This records your current balances as your starting point. At the end of the testing period, your second snapshot will show measurable change.

Section 1

What is Royal Ledger?

Royal Ledger is a financial discipline app — not a bank, not a budgeting spreadsheet, and not a subscription tracker. It is a structured system that helps you build a financial buffer, control impulse spending, allocate income intelligently, and develop consistent money habits over time.

The app is built around one central belief: financial freedom comes from discipline, not income size. It uses a stage-based buffer system, envelope budgeting, impulse gates, and daily checkpoints to give you the infrastructure to reach and maintain financial stability.

The core loop

  1. Set up your income, expenses, and buffer target during onboarding.
  2. Gate purchases using the Spending Gate before you spend, and log them after.
  3. Allocate income when it arrives — split it by stage rules to buffer, savings, trading capital, and lifestyle.
  4. Check in daily using the Command tab and Daily Checkpoints.
  5. Review weekly and monthly to close envelopes, take snapshots, and assess progress.

How it differs from other finance apps

Other appsRoyal Ledger
Connect to your bank, auto-import transactionsManual entry — intentional by design, no bank credentials ever requested
Track what you spent after the factGate purchases before they happen with a friction step
Show categories and pie chartsShow your stage, buffer progress, and whether you're on track structurally
Recommend stocks or investmentsNever — discipline system only, no financial advice given

Getting Started — Onboarding

When you first sign in, the app walks you through a one-time setup wizard. Complete every step carefully — most values can be adjusted later in Settings or Rules, but your income profile and currency are locked after setup.

  • 1
    Sign in with your invite code. Your code is tied to your email address. It expires 30 days after it is issued — use it promptly.
  • 2
    Choose your currency. Controls how all amounts are displayed — symbol, formatting, separators. Does not convert values. Choose carefully: locked after setup.
  • 3
    Choose your income profile. Foundation, Variable, Fixed, or Mixed. See Section 4 for the full comparison. Locked after setup.
  • 4
    Enter your monthly income / salary. Variable users enter an average or expected figure. Foundation users enter income or allowance. This is the reference number for all stage threshold calculations.
  • 5
    Enter your monthly fixed expenses. Rent, insurance, subscriptions, phone, bond — any committed cost that leaves your account every month regardless of your choices.
  • 6
    Set your buffer target. How many months of salary you want in your financial buffer. Default is 18 months. The system builds to this target step by step through the stages.
  • 7
    Enter your current buffer balance. What you actually have right now. This places you in the correct stage immediately.
  • 8
    Set a Security PIN. 4–6 digits. Protects structural settings from accidental changes. Optional but strongly recommended.
  • 9
    Done. The app calculates your stage and opens the Command tab.
Tester tip Try completing onboarding as different profiles in different browsers or private windows. Pay attention to whether each step is clear, whether the defaults make sense for each profile type, and whether error states (blank fields, invalid numbers) are handled gracefully.

Section 8

Budget Tab & Spending Gate

All profiles

The Budget tab combines two systems: envelope budgeting (how you manage categories) and the Spending Gate (how you control impulse decisions at the point of purchase).

Envelopes

Create named spending categories — Groceries, Fuel, Entertainment, Clothing, Eating Out. Each envelope has a monthly cap, an enforcement mode, and a rollover rule.

Block Mode — enforcement when the cap is reached

ModeWhat happens when you hit the cap
SoftApp warns you but still allows the purchase. You decide.
HardPurchase is refused. Cannot proceed until cap is increased or next month begins.
PIN overrideHard block, but you can bypass by entering your Security PIN. Forces a conscious, deliberate decision.

Rollover Mode — what happens to unspent balance at month-end

ModeWhat happens at the end of the month
ResetUse it or lose it. Unspent balance disappears; cap resets to full next month.
RolloverUnspent balance carries forward, increasing next month's available cap.
SweepUnspent balance moves to your buffer at month-end. Cap resets to full.

The Spending Gate

The Spending Gate is the app's core impulse-control mechanism. Any purchase at or above the gate threshold (default: 50 in your currency) triggers a structured decision flow before the purchase is logged. Purchases below the threshold skip the gate and are logged directly.

The gate flow — step by step

  • 1
    Name the item and enter the amount. What do you want to buy? How much does it cost?
  • 2
    Identify the emotional trigger. Why do you want it right now? Boredom? Stress? Reward? FOMO? Convenience? The app asks you to name it.
  • 3
    Choose the envelope the purchase comes from.
  • 4
    Make your decision. Three options are presented:
Skip it "Don't need it." — discards the purchase, logs nothing.
Sleep on it "Decide tomorrow." — parks the item for 24 hours, then prompts you to return and decide.
Buy now "I'm sure." — logs the purchase immediately to the selected envelope.

If you chose Sleep on it, the item sits in a pending queue. After 24 hours the app prompts you to return and make a final decision — at that point you can choose to log it or discard it.

Trading Guard Variable only

When a negative P&L is logged in the Trading tab, the Trading Guard activates automatically — no user action is required. For the next 24 hours, any purchase at or above the threshold is forced into the "Sleep on it" queue. The "Buy now" option is temporarily removed during this window. The guard expires on its own after 24 hours. A banner on the Command tab shows how long remains.

This prevents emotional spending after a trading loss — one of the most common destructive financial behaviours for active traders.

Tester tip Test all three gate options. Test Hard block mode — confirm it refuses at the cap. Test PIN override — wrong PIN must not bypass the block. Log a negative P&L (Trading tab) and confirm the Guard banner appears and "Buy now" is removed from the gate.
Section 9

History Tab

All profiles

The History tab is your financial archive. It stores monthly snapshots, expense records, impulse logs, and a net worth chart over time.

Monthly Snapshots

A snapshot is a point-in-time capture of your financial position, recording: date, buffer balance, trading capital, long-term savings, total assets (net worth), and salary reference.

Take a snapshot at month-end as part of your Monthly Review routine. Over time, snapshots form the data behind your net worth chart.

Net Worth Chart

A line chart plotting your total assets over every snapshot taken. This is the most honest measure of financial progress — it removes month-to-month noise and shows the long-term trajectory. Requires at least two snapshots to draw a line.

Expense History

Every purchase logged through the Spending Gate or Quick Log. Filter by month, category, or envelope. Use this to review actual spending patterns — not what you planned, what you actually did.

Impulse Log

A record of every Spending Gate decision: what you wanted to buy, the trigger you identified, whether you bought it or slept on it, and what you decided after the wait. Over time this becomes a powerful behavioural audit — you can see patterns in your own impulse triggers.

Tester tip Log several test expenses and impulses. Take at least two snapshots across different dates and verify the chart draws both points correctly. Reload the page and confirm records persist.

Trading P&L Tab

Variable

Visible only to Variable profile users. Tracks trading profit and loss by entry, month, and year.

Log P&L

After each trading session or at end of day, log your result — profit or loss — with a note. The entry is recorded against the current month.

If the amount logged is negative (a loss), the Trading Guard activates automatically for 24 hours — see Section 8. No separate action is required.

Monthly P&L Summary

Running total for the current month. When you run the Profit Allocator at month-end, this figure is used as the gross input. The tax reserve percentage (default 25%) is deducted first; the remainder is split per your stage rules.

P&L History

Month-by-month record of every result. Shows your best months, worst months, and overall trend.

Year-to-Date (YTD)

Running YTD total displayed here and as a quick figure on the Command tab. Resets at the start of each calendar year.

Tester tip Log a negative P&L and confirm: (1) the Trading Guard banner appears immediately on Command tab, (2) the "Buy now" button is absent from the Spending Gate for 24 hours, (3) the guard expires automatically. Then log a positive result and run the Profit Allocator — confirm tax is deducted before the split.

Section 14

Weekly Pulse & Monthly Review

Weekly Pulse

All profiles

A structured end-of-week review covering: spending this week vs. envelope caps, envelopes near or over limit, buffer movement, and trading P&L for the week (Variable/Mixed).

Triggered automatically on Sundays and manually at any time via the "Check Pulse" button on the Command tab. Resets weekly.

Monthly Review

All profiles

The most important ritual in the system. Do this at the end of every calendar month:

  • 1
    Roll envelopes. Apply each envelope's rollover rule. Sweep balances move to your buffer automatically.
  • 2
    Take a snapshot. Record your current buffer, capital, long-term, and net worth. Creates a data point on your History chart.
  • 3
    Run the Allocator. Enter this month's profit (Variable/Mixed) or surplus (Fixed). The Allocator splits it per your stage rules.
  • 4
    Log trading P&L for the month (Variable/Mixed). Confirm the final monthly figure before allocating.
  • 5
    Review and reset. Look at spending patterns and impulse log. Set your intention for the month ahead.

Section 16

Known Limitations

The following are deliberate design decisions in this version — not bugs. Please do not report them as issues. If you feel strongly that one of these should change, note it as feedback rather than a bug report.

LimitationWhy it's by design
No bank account integration Manual entry is intentional. Automatic import removes the friction and awareness that the system is built on. Connecting to a bank would undermine the discipline mechanic.
No automatic transaction categorisation You categorise purchases yourself at the Spending Gate. The act of categorising is part of the deliberate spending practice.
Single currency per account Currency is set at onboarding and locked. Multi-currency wallets are not in this version. All balances are in one currency.
No shared / couples accounts Each account is one user. Shared household budgeting is not in this version.
No native mobile app Royal Ledger is a PWA (web app installed to your home screen). It is not in the App Store or Play Store. Install from royalledger.app via your browser.
No investment recommendations The app tracks allocations to a "Long-term savings" bucket but does not recommend specific funds, stocks, or investment products. This is not a financial adviser.
No receipt scanning or OCR All amounts are entered manually. Receipt scanning is not in this version.
No recurring transaction tracking Fixed expenses are entered as a single monthly total in Setup, not as individual recurring items. Detailed subscription tracking is not in this version.
Section 17

Frequently Asked Questions

How is my data secured?
Your data is stored in Supabase (encrypted at rest, EU/US region). Your Security PIN is hashed locally using your email as salt — the raw PIN is never stored or transmitted. No bank credentials are ever requested. Cloud sync uses row-level security so only your account can read or write your data.
Can I use this on multiple devices?
Yes. Sign in with the same email on each device — data syncs automatically in the background. If you've made changes on one device and need them on another immediately, use Settings → Data & Sync → Upload local → cloud on the first device, then Download cloud → device on the second.
What happens to my data after the testing period ends?
You can keep using the app. Your data stays intact. If you choose to delete your account (Settings → Danger Zone), all data is wiped immediately. We do not delete tester accounts automatically after the 30-day period.
How is this different from YNAB, Mint, or 22seven?
Those tools track your spending after the fact and aggregate bank accounts. Royal Ledger is a discipline system — it gates purchases before they happen, uses a stage-based buffer model, and deliberately avoids bank integration. The goal is behavioural change, not categorised reporting.
I forgot my PIN — what do I do?
Open Settings → Security PIN → Forgot PIN? and submit a reset request with a short reason. Support reviews it and approves within 24 hours. You will be prompted to set a new PIN on your next login. Do not try to guess your PIN repeatedly — it has no lockout in this version, but incorrect guesses are logged.
My invite code says expired — what now?
Invite codes expire 30 days after they are issued. Email support@royalledger.app with your name and the email address you registered with — a new code will be issued within 24 hours.
Can I change my income profile or currency after setup?
Not from within the app — both are locked after onboarding to protect your historical data. Email support@royalledger.app with the change you need. Admin can update both remotely without data loss. Turnaround is typically within 24 hours.
What if I need to speak to someone urgently?
Email support@royalledger.app — replies within 24 hours during the testing period, usually faster. There is no live chat in this version.
Does the app work offline?
Partially. Installed as a PWA, the app loads and most features work offline. Changes made offline are queued and sync when connectivity returns. Cloud sync, backup upload, and PIN reset requests require internet.

Spending Gate & Budget

  • Log a purchase below 50 — it should skip the gate entirely.
  • Run the full gate on a purchase above 50 — verify all three buttons appear (Skip it, Sleep on it, Buy now).
  • Choose "Sleep on it" — confirm the item appears in a pending queue and a 24-hour timer is set.
  • Return after 24 hours and make a final decision on the parked item.
  • Test Hard block — create an envelope, cap it, spend to the cap, confirm it blocks.
  • Test PIN override — confirm wrong PIN does not bypass.
  • Run month-end rollover with a Sweep envelope and confirm balance moves to buffer.

How to report an issue

Email support@royalledger.app and include:

  1. What you were doing — which tab, which step, what action you took.
  2. What you expected — the intended result.
  3. What actually happened — the actual result, including any error message verbatim.
  4. Screenshot or screen recording — attach if possible; a picture resolves ambiguity faster than any description.
  5. Device and browser — e.g. iPhone 15 / Safari 17, Windows 11 / Chrome 124.
  6. Your income profile — Foundation, Variable, Fixed, or Mixed.
  7. Priority level — Critical, Important, or Nice to know (see top of this section).
Confusion reports are equally valuable Beyond bugs — anything that was hard to understand, a label that meant nothing, a step that felt wrong, or a moment where you had to re-read something twice. These reports shape the UX as much as bug reports do.
